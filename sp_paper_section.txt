6.z  Selective Prediction with Confidence Thresholding

6.z.1  Method

Selective prediction (also referred to as prediction with abstention)
augments a classifier with a rejection mechanism: a sample is accepted
for prediction only if the maximum softmax probability (MSP) meets or
exceeds a confidence threshold tau; otherwise the model abstains. We
evaluate four thresholds tau in {0.70, 0.80, 0.90, 0.95} on the
UT-HAR test set (n=500) using the trained ERM baseline. We report:

  - Accepted Accuracy: accuracy on the accepted subset
  - Coverage:          fraction of samples that receive a prediction
  - Abstention Rate:   fraction of samples that are rejected
  - Silent Failure Rate (SFR): P(wrong AND confident >= tau)

6.z.2  Results

  Baseline (ERM, no abstention): Accuracy = 98.40%

  tau    Accepted Acc   Coverage   Abst. Rate   SFR
  0.70   98.7928%       99.40%     0.60%        1.2000%
  0.80   99.3902%       98.40%     1.60%        0.6000%
  0.90   99.3865%       97.80%     2.20%        0.6000%
  0.95   99.5876%       97.00%     3.00%        0.4000%

6.z.3  Discussion

Increasing tau monotonically improves accepted accuracy (from 98.79%
at tau=0.70 to 99.59% at tau=0.95) and reduces the silent failure
rate (from 1.20% to 0.40%), at the cost of modest coverage reduction
(from 99.40% to 97.00%). Even at the strictest threshold (tau=0.95),
97% of test samples are still accepted, and accepted accuracy remains
below 100%, confirming that silent failures persist even at high
confidence. At tau=0.80-0.90, SFR plateaus at 0.60%, indicating a
residual set of misclassifications that the model assigns high
confidence regardless of threshold. This motivates the adoption of
conformal prediction (APS/RAPS) as a principled alternative that
provides formal coverage guarantees rather than heuristic thresholding.
